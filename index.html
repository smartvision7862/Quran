Biography of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W)

1. Birth and Early Life (ولادت اور ابتدائی زندگی)

Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W) was born in Mecca in the year 570 CE (Year of the Elephant) into the noble tribe of Quraysh. His father, Abdullah, passed away before his birth, and his mother, Aminah, passed away when he was only six years old. He was raised by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib, and later by his uncle Abu Talib. Even in his youth, he was renowned for his honesty, integrity, and trustworthiness, earning the titles of Al-Amin (the Trustworthy) and As-Sadiq (the Truthful).

2. The First Revelation (بعثت اور پہلی وحی)

At the age of forty, while meditating in the Cave of Hira on the Mount of Light (Jabal al-Nour), the Angel Gabriel (Jibril) appeared to him. The angel commanded him to read, conveying the first verses of the Holy Quran from Surah Al-Alaq: "Read in the name of your Lord who created..." This marked the beginning of his prophethood and the gradual revelation of the Quran over a period of 23 years.

3. The Migration to Madinah (ہجرتِ مدینہ)

After facing severe persecution, boycott, and trials in Mecca for thirteen years, Allah commanded the Prophet (S.A.W) and the early Muslims to migrate to Yathrib (later renamed Madinah). This migration, known as the Hijrah in 622 CE, marks the beginning of the Islamic Hijri calendar. In Madinah, he established a society based on brotherhood, justice, and the famous Covenant of Madinah, uniting various tribes.

4. High Character and Character Traits (اخلاقِ حسنہ)

The Quran describes the character of the Prophet (S.A.W) as a "sublime standard of character" (Surah Al-Qalam, 4:4). He was a mercy to all creation, showing unmatched kindness to children, the elderly, neighbors, and even his enemies. His life demonstrated humility, patience, generosity, and steadfastness in the face of adversity.

5. The Farewell Sermon (خطبہ حجتہ الوداع)

In the tenth year of Hijrah, during his final pilgrimage (Hajj), the Prophet (S.A.W) delivered his historic Farewell Sermon on Mount Arafat. He proclaimed the equality of all human beings, stating: "An Arab has no superiority over a non-Arab, nor a non-Arab has any superiority over an Arab; also a white has no superiority over a black, nor a black has any superiority over a white except by piety and good action." He completed his mission, leaving behind the Quran and Sunnah as a guide for humanity.
